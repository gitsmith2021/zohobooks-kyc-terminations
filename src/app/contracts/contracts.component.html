Agreement Terms & Conditions
1.INTRODUCTION. According to this Agreement, UCSS is responsible for implementing and maintaining the Unite EMR software on the Microsoft Azure Cloud, maintaining the database and application instance, and backing up the database and relevant files.
2.PERIOD OF AGREEMENT. Upon the Effective Date, this Agreement shall come into effect and continue for successive periods unless terminated. Either Party may terminate by giving at least thirty (30) days' written notice, effective on the last day of the Initial Period or any subsequent anniversary.
3.GOVERNMENT CHANGES. UCSS includes provision of enhancements related to government legislative changes, making efforts not to charge additional fees for such changes, except where the extent is significant.
4.INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS / CONFIDENTIALITY. Both parties commit to maintain confidentiality of any information related to the other's business. The customer agrees not to infringe upon any intellectual property rights owned by UCSS, including patents, copyrights, registered designs, or rights of confidence.
5.EXCLUSIONS. This Agreement does not include any implied warranties unless expressly stated. The customer's network security, anti-virus measures, ransomware protection, and backups within their premises are considered part of their infrastructure and are not included in this contract.
6.CONSEQUENTIAL LOSS. In no event shall either party be liable to the other for indirect or consequential loss or damage regardless of cause, or for loss of profits, business, revenue, goodwill, or anticipated savings.
7.TERMINATION. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately by providing written notice of thirty (30) days if either party fails to comply with its obligations, becomes bankrupt, insolvent, enters receivership, or ceases to carry on business.
8.FORCE MAJEURE. Neither party shall be held liable for failure or delay due to events beyond reasonable control including acts of God, government actions, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes, floods, fires, explosions, or natural calamities. If the Force Majeure Event lasts thirty (30) consecutive days or more, either party may terminate upon written notice.
9.WAIVER. Failure of one party to enforce any provision does not constitute a waiver of that provision. A waiver must be in writing and signed by both parties.
10.ASSIGNMENT. The Customer may not transfer, sell or rent any part of their interest in this Agreement or give any rights to a third party without UCSS's prior written approval.
11.NOTICES. Any notices shall be valid if in written form and delivered either by hand, prepaid letter, or email to the address specified in this Agreement.
12.HEADINGS. The headings of the clauses are provided for convenience only and shall not affect the meaning or construction of the said clauses.
13.SEVERABILITY. If any provision is found to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the validity of the remaining provisions shall not be affected. The parties shall make a good faith effort to replace the invalid provision with a valid one achieving the same purposes.
14.AMENDMENT. Any changes to this Agreement can only be made through a written Agreement signed by the authorized representatives of both parties.
15.GOVERNING LAW. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Any dispute shall be referred to the courts of Dubai, United Arab Emirates.
16. Obligations of the Customer
1.The Customer shall be responsible for mobile devices, desktops, laptops, printers, and internet connectivity within their facility. Failure of the internet will cause interruption of application access.
2.The Customer will stay "Active" and "Enrolled" by paying the annual subscription on or before the commencement of the service year. Failure to pay on time will lead to service disruption.
3.Upon detection of any error or defect, the Customer shall submit a support ticket through the Help Desk or in-app chat with all relevant data. The Authorized Service Contact (ASC) should be the person raising the call. Requests through other channels shall not be considered official.
4.The Customer shall provide UCSS with ample computer time to locate and remedy defects.
5.The Customer shall provide remote access to enable UCSS staff to carry out necessary Implementation when required.
6.Customer shall provide necessary downtime to UCSS for the deployment of Software patches or upgrades whenever necessary.
7.The Customer must not make any modifications to the licensed product/database structure provided by UCSS without prior written consent from UCSS.
8.On receiving a fix from UCSS, the Customer is to confirm acceptance of the "Fix" and closure in writing to UCSS.
9.Customer shall appoint an "Authorized Support Contact" (ASC) to interact with the UCSS team for onboarding, training, go-live, and subsequent support management.
10.Within 5 working days of signing the contract, the customer shall provide all necessary inputs and data to the UCSS onboarding team for setting up the database.
11.Upon signing the contract, the customer is expected to implement Unite EMR and go-live within two weeks. UCSS allows for one extension request granting an additional two-week period.
12.To comply with government regulations, the customer must ensure that all users utilize the application for appropriate data entry purposes.
17. Obligation of UCSS — Service Level Agreement (SLA)
1.Availability Guarantee: UCSS guarantees an uptime of at least 99.9% each month, excluding scheduled maintenance and force majeure events.
2.Support Response Time: UCSS will respond to support tickets within 2 business days upon receipt, providing either a resolution or an estimated timeframe.
3.Escalation Procedures: If a support ticket is not resolved within the initial response time, it will be escalated to a higher level of support until a resolution is achieved.
4.Maintenance Schedule: UCSS will notify the customer in advance of any scheduled maintenance causing service interruption, performed during off-peak hours whenever possible.
5.Backup and Disaster Recovery: UCSS will maintain a regular backup schedule to ensure data integrity and will have a disaster recovery plan in place to minimize downtime.
6.Reporting and Monitoring: UCSS will monitor the service for performance issues and notify the customer of any potential issues.
7.Support Channels: Support ticket via Help Desk and/or in-app chat  ·  On-site (emergency): +971-54 704 2150 / +971-50 689 2152  ·  Escalation: latha.ns@unite.care

9.Data transfer upon termination: In the unlikely event of contract termination, UCSS will provide patient demographics, medical records, and billing details in a format determined by UCSS. The format may change to accommodate evolving data structures.
10.UCSS shall be contractually bound to implement and go-live with Unite EMR for the customer in accordance with the Statement of Work (SOW). Any breach by either party may lead to contract termination. If terminated prior to go-live, the customer will not be responsible for subscription fees.
18. Scope of Works Covered Under This Agreement
1.Schedule an onboarding meeting with the client representative(s) to map customer processes to Unite EMR processes.
2.Collect the necessary data to set up a customer-specific database for use in the Unite EMR environment.
3.The modules as mentioned in Appendix 1 are included in the package purchased by the customer.
4.Configure and deploy the software modules included in the scope for one legal entity in the English language.
5.All user training shall be provided through Unitecare Learning Management System (LMS), including end-to-end training modules for relevant users identified by the Customer.
6.Access to the Unite LMS shall be provided for a validity period of three (3) working days from the date the LMS link is shared.
7.The Customer shall ensure all relevant users complete the training within the LMS access period.
8.Online go-live support shall be provided for a total duration not exceeding eight (8) hours, over a maximum span of two (2) business days.
9.Any additional training or support beyond the scope defined above shall be provided remotely and may be subject to mutually agreed commercial terms.
10.All documentation will be shared as video guides or short clips based on need.
19. Out of Scope — Not Covered Under This Agreement

All "Out of Scope" work, if requested, will be delivered on Time and Materials (T&M) basis.

1.Additional customizations and integrations/interfaces beyond those agreed in this contract.
2.Additional on-site training exceeding the 4-hour limit specified in this Agreement.
3.Additional post-go-live support exceeding the 8-hour limit.
4.Migration of historical transaction data from external systems.
5.Purchase of a payment gateway account or SMS package.
6.Integrations with SMS providers other than Elite Buzz & Axiom ME.
7.Implementation of wireless functionalities, integration with new 3rd party software/hardware (except as agreed), VPN/LAN/leased line setup, and lab equipment integration.

1.The pricing and any other charges arising under this Agreement shall be payable in accordance with the Standard Terms as stated in Appendix 3 of this Agreement.
2.All prices quoted are in AED (Arab Emirates Dirhams) unless otherwise specified.
