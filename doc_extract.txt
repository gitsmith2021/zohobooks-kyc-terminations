OverviewThis document defines the functional specification for the Zoho Contract & KYC Management System. The system manages the complete customer contract lifecycle, including customer onboarding, KYC collection and verification, contract preparation and signing, amendment handling, termination processing, and automated data handover.The system integrates three operational layers:Customer Dashboard – self-service portal for KYC submission, contract viewing, and termination requestsInternal Contract Management Portal – used by the Contract Team, Sales, Operations, and Accounts teamsAutomated Infrastructure Layer – executes scheduled deactivation, backup, and handover tasks2.  Contract Team DashboardThe Contract Team Dashboard provides a centralised, real-time view of all contract-related activities. It serves as the primary workspace for the Contract Team to monitor and act on customer records.2.1  Customer View – Status SegmentsThe dashboard groups customers by their current contract status. Each segment is filterable and searchable:Contract Sent – customers who have been issued a contract awaiting signatureContract Signed – customers with a fully executed contract on recordContract Pending – customers in progress (KYC approved; contract not yet dispatched)Termination Requested – customers who have initiated a termination requestContract Terminated – customers whose contracts have been fully closed2.2  Dashboard FeaturesView and download customer-related documents (KYC files, signed contracts)Track contract status changes with timestampsView KYC approval history and verification notesReview amendment history per customerAccess notification logs for all system-triggered communicationsSupport Team Dashboard – Terminated customer records (customer name, contract end date, termination reason, final status) must be displayed on the Support Team Dashboard so that support agents can handle post-termination queries correctly and identify terminated customers in real time3.  Customer Onboarding & KYC Workflow3.1 Onboarding Fee PaymentThe KYC workflow is triggered automatically once the customer completes the onboarding fee payment. No manual intervention is required from the Contract Team at this stage.PROCESS FLOWCustomer completes onboarding fee payment via the payment gatewaySystem validates payment confirmationSystem generates a secure, time-limited KYC form linkSystem sends the KYC form link to the customer via Email and WhatsApp (optional)System updates the customer status to KYC Form SentSystem dispatches an internal notification to required staffSTATUS UPDATEDKYC Form Sent3.1A KYC Expiry – Email Notification to Expired CustomersThe Contract Team Dashboard must provide a bulk email option for customers whose KYC has expired or is approaching the expiry date.PROCESSDashboard displays a filterable list of KYC-Expired customersContract Team selects one or more expired-KYC customers using a multi-select checkboxContract Team clicks “Send KYC Renewal Email” to dispatch renewal reminder emails in bulkSystem sends individual renewal emails to each selected customer with a fresh KYC form linkAll bulk email actions are recorded in the audit log with acting user, timestamp, and recipient list3.2 Secure KYC Form AccessThe KYC form must be accessed via an authenticated, secure link to protect customer data and prevent unauthorised submissions.SECURITY REQUIREMENTSOne-time secure URL – each link is valid for a single access sessionTime-limited expiry – links expire after a configurable periodKYC Expiry Date / Next Renewal Date – Contract Team must set an Expiry Date or Next Renewal Date on each KYC Form at time of issuance. This date is mandatory and tracked by the system. Records approaching or past expiry are flagged on the Contract Team Dashboard.Customer identity verification via registered Email or Mobile OTP before form accessAll document uploads must be encrypted in transit and at restAudit log entry created on each link access attemptℹ Expired or reused links must display a clear error message and prompt the customer to contact support.3.3 KYC Form SubmissionOnce authenticated, the customer completes the KYC form and uploads all required documents.CUSTOMER ACTIONSFill all mandatory KYC fieldsUpload required supporting documentsREQUIRED DOCUMENTS (CONFIGURABLE)Trade LicencePassport CopyEmirates IDVAT CertificateAddress ProofOther supporting documents (as configured per customer type)SYSTEM ACTIONS ON SUBMISSIONUpdate customer status to KYC SubmittedNotify the Contract Management Team via internal notificationStore all uploaded files securely in the document repositorySTATUS UPDATEDKYC Submitted4. Contract Management5.1 Common Contract Template – Customer DashboardA standard contract template is available for customers to preview at any time via the Customer Dashboard. This allows customers to familiarise themselves with contract terms before the formal contract is issued.Display the common contract template in read-only formatAllow customers to download a copy of the template for reference5.2 Customer-Specific AmendmentsBefore dispatching the final contract for signature, the Contract Team may add customer-specific amendments. These amendments are unique per customer and are appended to the standard template.AMENDMENT EXAMPLESCustom pricing or discount termsSpecial service conditions or SLAsAdditional clauses for regulated industriesService-specific conditions based on the subscribed productℹ Amendments must be recorded in the Amendment History log with the author and timestamp.6. Automated Termination ProcessOn reaching the agreed termination date, the system automatically executes the following four steps without requiring manual intervention. All steps are logged in the audit trail.Accounts Clearance Gate: Before the Automated Termination Process is triggered, the Accounts Team must provide explicit Accounts Clearance confirming all dues are settled. If the customer has any pending amount, the system blocks automated termination, raises an alert to the Contract Team, and sets the status to “Termination – Accounts Pending”. An authorised override option is available for exceptional cases; the override requires a reason to be recorded and is fully audit-logged.Step 1DATA HANDOVER PREPARATION Gather all customer data files, compress into a ZIP archive, store to Blob Storage, and generate a time-limited secure download URL.Step 2DATABASE BACKUP & CLEANUP Create a full backup of the customer database, store the backup to Blob Storage, and permanently delete the live customer database after successful backup verification.Step 3IIS APPLICATION DEACTIVATION Inactivate the customer-specific IIS application instance and stop all associated application services.Step 4CORE URL DEACTIVATION Disable the customer's domain or subdomain access and inactivate all core application URLs associated with the customer account.10.  Accounts Settlement & Data Handover10.1 Settlement Clearance – Accounts TeamBefore the data handover email is sent to the customer, the Accounts Team must confirm that all pending dues and financial obligations have been settled.ACCOUNTS TEAM ACTIONSVerify all pending invoices and outstanding duesApprove settlement clearance in the portalSettlement PendingSettlement Cleared10.2 Data Handover EmailOnce the Accounts Team approves settlement clearance, the system sends the data handover email to the customer.Customer-Only Access Restriction: The data download link is exclusively for the customer. No internal user (Contract Team, Accounts Team, Sales, or Support) may open or utilise the link. The system must enforce customer-identity verification (e.g. OTP or registered email authentication) before permitting any download. Any internal access attempt must be blocked and recorded in the audit log as an access violation.EMAIL CONTENTSecure download link for the customer's data ZIP archiveLink expiry date and timeDownload instructions and support contactℹ  The download link is time-limited. If the link expires before the customer downloads their data, the Accounts Team can regenerate and resend the link (see Section 11).11.  Resend Download Link FeatureTo handle cases where the original download link has expired, the Accounts Team is provided a self-service feature to regenerate and resend the handover link.PROCESSAccounts Team identifies the expired link in the customer's recordAccounts Team triggers "Resend Download Link" action in the portalSystem generates a new secure token and a fresh time-limited URLOld link is automatically invalidatedSystem sends the new data handover email to the customerSECURITY CONTROLSEach resend action generates a brand-new token – old links cannot be reactivatedAll resend actions are recorded in the audit log with the acting user and timestampResend access is restricted to authorised Accounts Team members only12. The team adds customer-specific amendments, then follows one of two paths: send after subscription payment (Approach 1) or send immediately with a disclaimer (Approach 2, authorised override). Once signed, the contract is stored and displayed in the dashboard.Phase 3 — Termination & Handover handles the full off-boarding lifecycle: request → review → approval → four automated system steps (data ZIP, DB backup, IIS stop, URL disable) → accounts settlement clearance → data handover email with a time-limited download link. If the link expires, the Accounts Team can regenerate and resend it without manual dev work.Discussion Call -18-05-2026->Call-Off Termination -With Remark any stage->Download link - with device details->Send Mail To Customer - For Data Deletion Confirmation->Read Me-For Data-Discussion Call Notes – Paragraph DraftsCall-off termination should be supported as a controlled action at any stage of the termination workflow, based on authorised internal review. When this action is used, the user must enter a mandatory remark explaining the reason for stopping, cancelling, or reversing the termination flow. The system should record the remark, acting user, date, and current stage in the audit log so that the termination history remains fully traceable.The customer data download link should capture device-related access details whenever the link is opened or used. This includes information such as device type, browser, IP address, access timestamp, and download status, subject to system availability and security policy. These details should be stored in the audit trail to strengthen traceability, support security monitoring, and help validate that the handover link was accessed only by the intended customer.After the data handover process is completed, the system should send an email to the customer requesting confirmation for data deletion. This communication should clearly state that the customer data has been made available for download, specify the confirmation action required from the customer, and define the expected response timeline if applicable. Once the customer confirms, the system should update the record accordingly and maintain the confirmation event in the audit log for compliance and reference purposes.A read-me file or guidance note should be included as part of the customer data handover package to help the customer understand the delivered files. This note should briefly explain the folder structure, file categories, backup contents, access considerations, and any important usage instructions relevant to the handed-over data. Providing this guidance will reduce confusion, improve usability of the delivered package, and support a smoother closure process during termination.
